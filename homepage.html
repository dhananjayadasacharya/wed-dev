Landslides

Landslides are the downward movement of rock, soil, and debris under the influence of gravity, often triggered by heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, or human activities such as construction and mining. These natural disasters can occur rapidly and with devastating force, leading to significant property damage, infrastructure destruction, and loss of life. Landslides can vary in scale from small rockfalls to massive avalanches of earth and rock, depending on factors like the slope's steepness, the type of soil, and the amount of moisture present. Understanding and monitoring landslide-prone areas, implementing effective land use planning, and employing engineering solutions can help mitigate their impact and improve community resilience to these potentially destructive events.

Natural Disasters

Natural disasters can strike at any time. It is important to be prepared and know how to respond. Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, and wildfires are among the most common disasters that affect communities worldwide. Stay informed and follow the advice of local authorities to stay safe.
